I am Suyash Nayan, a protocol and systems engineer with most of my recent work in Ethereum execution-layer infrastructure, MEV systems, transaction propagation, and performance-sensitive backend tooling.

The short version: I like hard systems problems where correctness, latency, and messy real-world constraints all show up at once. I have shipped 25+ open-source PRs across Reth, Hyperledger Besu, and Flashbots Rbuilder; reduced transaction latency by 61% in Besu private networks during an LFX mentorship; built Rust tools for AMM simulation and Ethereum P2P mempool ingestion; and presented blockchain performance work at Open Source Summit Japan in Tokyo.

Right now I am deliberately turning that systems background toward AI and ML infrastructure. I am especially interested in inference engineering, model-serving systems, GPU-aware backends, evaluation pipelines, and the machinery that makes intelligent software fast, observable, and reliable. I am building small AI/ML exercises in public as a bridge: not to cosplay as an ML researcher, but to learn the stack from the bottom up and connect it to what I already know well.

The throughline is still systems work. Ethereum clients taught me how to reason about peer-to-peer networking, async systems, transaction pools, benchmarking, and adversarial production environments. AI infrastructure has a different surface area, but the same kind of engineering taste matters: isolate bottlenecks, understand the runtime, measure honestly, and build tools that survive contact with real traffic.

I am currently looking for roles around AI inference, ML systems, backend infrastructure, and performance engineering. Crypto is where I built my strongest public proof; AI/ML infra is where I am aiming the next chapter.

**What I bring**

- Rust, Go, Java, C++, TypeScript, Python, across systems, backend, and protocol code.
- Execution-layer and networking experience from Reth, Besu, and Flashbots Rbuilder contributions.
- Async pipeline work with Tokio, PostgreSQL, WebSockets, benchmarking harnesses, and observability surfaces.
- A bias toward building real tools, writing up the work, and making the evidence easy to inspect.

**What I am exploring now**

- Inference serving, batching, latency/throughput tradeoffs, and model runtime internals.
- Practical ML projects that force me to implement data flow, evaluation, deployment, and monitoring.
- How performance engineering lessons from blockchain clients transfer into AI systems.
